# Incubators & Accelerators

## Top Incubators & Accelerators in Sydney

1. Startmate | https://www.startmate.com/

-   Premier Australian accelerator for early-stage tech -- historically offers around AUD$75k for 7% equity over a 3-month program with deep mentor networks and investor access.
-   Ideal for structured programs, funding, and investor introductions.

2. INCUBATE (University of Sydney) | https://incubate.org.au/

-   Award-winning university-affiliated accelerator offering pre-accelerator, accelerator, community, mentorship, funding advice, and support networks. Over 220 startups accelerated and strong outcomes.
-   Excellent if you're affiliated with USYD. Low-cost/high-value mentorship and validation support.
-   This is a pre-accelerators (very early ideas) and accelerator (for MVP+)
-   Benefits are: access to mentors, alumni founders, and investors; workshops on product, growth, fundraising, legal, and pitching; strong credibility -- many INCUBATE alumni have gone on to raise significant rounds.
-   With a working MVP, likely be eligible for Accelerator stream.

What you get... Sharpening + credibility + connections

-   Structured Accelerator program (usually ~14 weeks).
-   Mentorship from founders, operators, investors.
-   Workshops: product-market fit, fundraising, GTM, legal, scaling.
-   Community of founders at similar stage.
-   A Demo Day with real investors + angels.
-   Sometimes small stipends or AWS/Google/Azure credits (not as big as YC/Techstars though).

3. Genesis | https://www.sydney.edu.au/business/study/student-experience/sydney-genesis.html

-   What it is: A cohort-based program with workshops, weekly “goal nights,” mentoring, and a final pitch night.
-   Focus: Helping founders refine strategy, validate business models, and practice pitching.
-   Alumni eligibility: Also open to alumni, staff, and current students.
-   Why it’s a fit: Genesis is a lighter lift than INCUBATE — excellent if you want to sharpen your pitch and get structured feedback before applying to bigger accelerators like Startmate or muru-D.

What you get:

-   Weekly workshops & Goal nights
-   1:1 coaching with mentors
-   A pitch night finale

4. Cicada Innovations | https://www.cicadainnovations.com/

-   Mentorship, peer support, pitch practice, VC introductions, global forums, and events. Great for founder networking and informal guidance
-   Great if the product aligns with their focus (e.g., AI, deep tech, fintech)

## US / Global Accelerators and Support

1. Y Combinator (YC):

-   The gold standard (US$500K standard deal).
-   Extremely competitive, but your B2B/B2D focus + working MVP + real-time AI infra is attractive.
-   Wants startups that can be massive companies (multi-billion potential)
-   love platforms / infra because if you become the 'default' layer for something, upside is enormous.
-   don't mind if it looks niche at first (Stripe started with 'payments for devs', Twilio wiht 'SMS APIs', OpenAI with 'a research org')

2. Techstars:

-   Global program with 50+ city chapters. Some accept fully remote participation.
-   Offers ~US$120K funding + mentorship.
-   Broader than Y combinator, will accept strong B2B SaaS and dev tools.
-   Run many local programs (some vertical-specific: AI, Fintech, Sustainability).

3. Sequoia Arc, a16z Speedrun, Alchemist Accelerator (more niche):

-   _Alchemist_, in particular, is strong for B2B/developer infrastructure startups.

Alchemist

-   Very focused on B2B and technical founders.
-   perfect for things like APIs, SDKs, dev platforms, and enterprise infra.
-   Schma's voice-to-function parsing infra fits squarely in their sweet spot.
-   They don't require consumer-scale traction, just strong technical proof and some early B2B demand.

## Provider startups

1. Google for Startups (Cloud Program) | https://cloud.google.com/startup?hl=en

-   typically offer US$2,000-$200,000 in Google Cloud Credits (tier depends on stage and partner)
-   access to technical support, training, and co-marketing opportunities.
-   strong fit since I'm building on Google STT (fallback) and Gemini (main LLM provider)
